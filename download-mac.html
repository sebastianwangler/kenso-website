First launch: macOS Gatekeeper

On first launch, macOS may show a dialog like this:

"KENSO" cannot be opened because the developer cannot be verified. macOS cannot verify that this app is free from malware.

Don't worry — simply follow these steps:

  1. 1 Right-click (or Control-click) on KENSO.app
  2. 2 Select Open from the context menu
  3. 3 Click Open in the dialog that appears
  4. 4 KENSO will launch — macOS remembers your choice for future starts

Why does this happen?

KENSO is notarized by Apple, which confirms the app is free from malware. However, because KENSO is not a native Mac app but an R-based application packaged for macOS, Gatekeeper may still show a warning on first launch. KENSO is built by an independent developer — the app is safe to run, it runs entirely on your Mac and your data never leaves the machine.

Troubleshooting

  • If right-click → Open doesn't work: go to System Settings → Privacy & Security → scroll down → click "Open Anyway" next to the KENSO message
  • Make sure you drag KENSO.app to your Applications folder before opening
